fixes

Candle making does not always go perfectly. Most problems are common, and many can be improved with small adjustments to temperature, pouring, cooling, or wick choice.

tunneling

Tunneling happens when the candle burns down the centre instead of melting across the full top surface.

This is often caused by a wick that is too small or by not allowing the candle to burn long enough on the first burn.

frosting

Frosting appears as pale or white crystal patterns on soy wax. This is natural and does not mean the candle is ruined.

It can often be reduced by pouring at a better temperature and letting the candle cool slowly.

wet spots

Wet spots happen when the wax pulls away from the inside of the jar as it cools.

They are usually caused by temperature changes while cooling. Warming the jars slightly before pouring can help.

sinkholes

Sinkholes can appear near the wick as the wax cools and contracts.

A second small pour or gentle heat can help smooth the surface.

rough tops

Rough or uneven tops often happen when wax cools too quickly or the pour temperature is off.

Slower cooling and more controlled pouring can help create a smoother finish.

scent issues

If the scent feels too weak, the fragrance load, curing time, or mixing temperature may need adjusting.

If the scent feels too strong, the fragrance percentage may be too high.

mould topper problems

If wax toppers crack, break, or do not stick properly, make sure they are fully cooled before demoulding and slightly melt the base before pressing them onto the candle.
