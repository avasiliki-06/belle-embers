materials

These are the main materials and tools used to make Belle Embers candles, from preparing the jars to pouring the wax and adding decorative toppers.

wax

Wax is the base of the candle. For example, 4 candles in 8 oz jars would need about 32 oz of wax.

jars or vessels

Jars hold the candle and affect the final look. Leave a little space at the top if you plan to add mould toppers later.

wicks

Wicks are attached to the jars using sticky tabs or a glue gun. A wick centering tool helps keep them straight while the wax sets.

fragrance oil

Fragrance gives the candle its scent. Around 5% fragrance creates a lighter scented candle, while 7% creates a stronger and more concentrated scent.

thermometer

A thermometer is used to check the wax temperature while melting, cooling, and pouring. This helps the wax and fragrance combine properly.

scale

A scale is used to measure both the wax and the fragrance oil accurately.

pouring pot and stirring tool

A pot or pouring pitcher is used to melt the wax, and a stirring tool is needed to mix in fragrance and dye evenly for about 2 minutes.

dropper and moulds

A dropper helps fill small moulds neatly with leftover wax. Decorative moulds can be hearts, bunnies, or other small shapes used for candle toppers.

wick trimmer and extras

A wick trimmer is used at the end to trim the wick. Glitter can also be added as an optional final detail.
